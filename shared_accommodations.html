Trends in accommodation prices in San Francisco

Co-authors: Grace Bai, Janel Chadiarova, Anson Leong, Rochie Cuevas

San Francisco is one of the most visited cities in the USA. It attracts about 25 million visitors, with tourism being its biggest industry. With many visitors streaming in, residents have started renting out their spaces for short durations for extra income and use peer-to-peer property rental platforms such as Airbnb to list their available spaces. Therefore, there are four main types of accommodations considered in this report: owned homes, long-term rental properties, peer-to-peer short-term rental properties, and hotels. This study explores trends in prices for these accommodation types in San Francisco.

Data on accommodation rates in the different neighbourhoods of San Francisco are obtained from different sources:

  • DataSF, home prices
  • Zillow, long-term rental rates
  • SF City Performance Scorecards, hotel rates
  • Inside Airbnb, short-term rental rates

The data from the different sources are cleaned, processed, and visualised using Python scripts written in different jupyter notebooks.

Home Prices

Results indicate that the housing price was highest in 2011 and 2016, on average, in the Financial District while the most affordable neighbourhoods to own a home were Silver Tree and Visitacion Valley.


Long-term rent

It is notable that long-term rental rates in the Financial District were not as different as the other neighbourhoods. Three neighbourhoods popped out having higher long-term rental rates in 2011 and in 2016: Presidio Heights, Sea Cliff, and St. Francis Wood. Sea Cliff and St. Francis Wood are classified as "residence parks". Presidio Heights took the top spot for rental rates; these could be attributed to its proximity to nature despite its closeness to the hustle and bustle of the City.


The trends in price of accommodations increased from 2011 to 2016. However, the rates of increases are different. The home price increases were more gradual than that for long-term rental rate. From 2012 to 2015, the increase in long-term rental rates was steep; but the increases seemed to slow down in 2015–2016.

Hotel rates

Hotel rates also went up between 2005 and 2017. There was a drop in hotel rates between 2008 and 2009, but this can probably be attributed to the Recession during that time. Since 2009, however, the hotel rates grew at a rate faster then in 2005–2008. In 2015–2017, the data suggests that the increase in hotel rates is slowing down.

Short-term rent

The slowing growth trend for both hotel and long-term rental rates might be an effect of the entry of peer- to-peer short-term rental options. The short-term rental rates appeared to by highest in Presidio and Presidio Heights, mimicking patterns of long-term rental rates. The rates were lowest in Crocker Amazon in 2015 and 2016; but by 2017, the rates there have overtaken those of Excelsior, Treasure Island/Yerba Buena Island, and Visitacion Valley. Crocker Amazon and Excelsior are described as ethnically diverse residential areas, with single-family homes. Treasure Island/Yerba Buena Island, on the other hand, feature only rental housing. In 2018, all short-term rental listings for Treasure Island have been wiped out because the agency handling rentals does not allow vacation rentals.

In terms of districts, Western Addition and Outside Lands experienced a continuous increase in short-term rental rates. In contrast, there was a price decrease from 2016 to 2017 in Downtown, in North of Downtown, and in Southern districts. The number of listings of peer-to-peer short-term rental clearly increased from 2015 to 2017. In 2015 and 2016, Outside Lands had the fewest listings for peer-to-peer short-term property rental. By 2017, however, the district already had the same number of listings as North of Downtown. On the other hand, Western Addition had the most number of listings for the three years.

Zooming into the neighbourhood level, it is clear that Mission, South of Market, and Western Addition had the most number of listings for peer-to-peer short-term rental units across the three years. On the other hand, Presidio and Treasure Island had fewest listings.

Heatmaps show the progression in the number of short-term rental listings and rates. It is interesting to note that there were two regions of particularly high rates for short-term rental properties(northern part of the City and near Twin Peaks); however, the rates spiked for most neighbourhoods in the next two years. The rates rose faster around the Downtown and North of Downtown areas than around Twin Peaks. On the other hand, there was only one hotspot for short-term rental listings in 2015. It was concentrated around the central part of the City; however, the listings quickly spread in the subsequent years, with a second concentration growing around the Downtown and South of Market areas.
